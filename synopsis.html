Project Synopsis

The project aims to build CyPhyHouse--a framework for rapid development and deployment of distributed, heterogeneous, and mobile robotic systems. This new infrastructure will provide easy to use, open source, software and hardware platform for rapidly programming, simulating, deploying, and visualizing a rich family of applications on heterogeneous fleet of vehicles. It will enable experimental research at scale needed for developing computing systems that support and rely on coordinated and controlled mobility. CyPhyHouse will enable a variety of new research programs broadly pertaining to computing systems that support or rely on controlled and coordinated mobility. Examples include, a study on coordinated and mobile infrastructure (e.g., WiFi access points); development of practical debuggers for distributed cyber-physical systems; research on consistency models for distributed shared memory that are optimized for swarm robotics; and real-time system identification techniques for distributed cyber-physical systems (CPS). To achieve this vision we have the following major goals:

  • Development of high-level programming language abstraction and interface
  • Development of supporting software: compilers, simulators, loaders, data loggers and visualization
  • Development of compliant hardware platforms
  • Engagement of UIUC ECE/CS community for getting feedback on and showcasing the capabilities of CyPhyHouse towards creation of new research projects using the infrastructure.
